Pizza Sales Analysis using SQL

This project focuses on analyzing a fictional pizza company's sales data using SQL in MySQL Workbench to uncover meaningful business insights and support data-driven decision-making. The dataset consisted of multiple relational tables containing information on orders, order details, pizza types, and pricing. I wrote a series of structured SQL queries to explore various aspects of business performance, such as total orders placed, overall revenue generated, top-selling pizza types, most popular pizza sizes, and peak ordering hours. I also performed category-wise breakdowns, calculated average pizzas sold per day, and used window functions to analyze cumulative revenue and identify the top-performing pizzas by revenue within each category. The project demonstrates my skills in data extraction, joining multiple tables, applying aggregate functions, using subqueries, and working with advanced SQL techniques like ranking functions and time-based analysis. It reflects my ability to cleanly structure queries to answer practical business questions and provide insights that can help optimize operations, marketing, and product decisions.
